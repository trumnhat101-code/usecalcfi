How federal income tax is calculated

Federal income tax uses progressive brackets — you pay a different rate on each portion of your income. For 2025:

  • 10% on income up to $11,925 (single) / $23,850 (married)
  • 12% on $11,925–$48,475 (single) / $23,850–$96,950 (married)
  • 22% on $48,475–$103,350 (single) / $96,950–$206,700 (married)
  • 24% on $103,350–$197,300 (single) / $206,700–$394,600 (married)
  • 32% on $197,300–$250,525 (single) / $394,600–$501,050 (married)
  • 35% on $250,525–$626,350 (single) / $501,050–$751,600 (married)
  • 37% on income above those thresholds

The standard deduction ($15,000 single / $30,000 MFJ in 2025) is subtracted before applying brackets. Your pre-tax deductions (401k, HSA, etc.) further reduce your taxable income.

What is FICA tax?

FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act) funds Social Security and Medicare:

  • Social Security: 6.2% on wages up to $176,100 (2025 wage base)
  • Medicare: 1.45% on all wages (+ 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax over $200,000 for single filers)

Your employer matches these contributions — so the full FICA cost is actually 15.3% of your wages, split equally between you and your employer.

States with no income tax

If you live in Alaska, Florida, Nevada, New Hampshire, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, or Wyoming, enter 0% for state tax. Note that some of these states have other taxes (sales, property) that may be higher to compensate.

Frequently asked questions

What are pre-tax deductions?

Pre-tax deductions are contributions taken from your gross pay before taxes are calculated — reducing your taxable income. Common examples include 401(k) or 403(b) contributions, employer-sponsored health/dental/vision insurance premiums, HSA contributions, and FSA contributions.

What is the difference between tax bracket and effective tax rate?

Your marginal rate is the bracket your highest dollar of income falls into. Your effective rate is the average across all brackets — always lower than your marginal rate. This calculator shows your effective total tax rate.

How can I increase my take-home pay?

Maximize pre-tax contributions (401k, HSA, FSA) to reduce federal and state taxable income. Review your W-4 to ensure you're not over-withholding. If you consistently get a large refund, adjusting your W-4 allowances gives you that money in each paycheck instead.
