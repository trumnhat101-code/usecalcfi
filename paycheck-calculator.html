How to use this paycheck calculator

Select salary or hourly pay, enter your pay frequency, and choose your federal filing status. Add eligible pre-tax payroll deductions and a state tax rate estimate. If you want a closer federal estimate, open the W-4 section and enter the annual dollar amounts from Steps 3, 4(a), 4(b), or the extra amount per paycheck from Step 4(c).

The result shows each deduction per paycheck and per year, your estimated federal taxable income, effective tax rate, take-home percentage, and a pay-frequency comparison.

2026 federal tax brackets used

Federal income tax uses progressive brackets: only the dollars inside a bracket are taxed at that bracket's rate. This calculator uses these 2026 taxable-income thresholds:

  • 10% up to $12,400 (single) / $24,800 (married filing jointly)
  • 12% from $12,400–$50,400 (single) / $24,800–$100,800 (married)
  • 22% from $50,400–$105,700 (single) / $100,800–$211,400 (married)
  • 24% from $105,700–$201,775 (single) / $211,400–$403,550 (married)
  • 32% from $201,775–$256,225 (single) / $403,550–$512,450 (married)
  • 35% from $256,225–$640,600 (single) / $512,450–$768,700 (married)
  • 37% on income above those thresholds

The 2026 standard deduction is $16,100 for single filers, $32,200 for married couples filing jointly, and $24,150 for heads of household. The calculator subtracts it before applying the brackets.

Social Security and Medicare taxes in 2026

FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act) funds Social Security and Medicare:

  • Social Security: 6.2% of employee wages up to the $184,500 wage base.
  • Medicare: 1.45% of all employee wages, with no wage cap.
  • Additional Medicare withholding: employers withhold another 0.9% from wages they pay above $200,000 in a calendar year.

Your employer generally matches the 6.2% Social Security and 1.45% Medicare amounts. The employer match is not deducted from your pay. A traditional 401(k) contribution usually reduces federal taxable income but not Social Security or Medicare wages; some Section 125 benefits can reduce both.

How the estimate is calculated

  1. Annualize salary or hourly wages and the selected pay frequency.
  2. Subtract eligible pre-tax deductions and the 2026 standard deduction; include optional W-4 Step 4(a) income and Step 4(b) deductions.
  3. Apply 2026 federal brackets, subtract Step 3 credits, and add Step 4(c) extra withholding.
  4. Calculate employee Social Security and Medicare taxes, then add the flat state-rate estimate.
  5. Subtract taxes and pre-tax payroll deductions from gross pay to estimate take-home pay.

This method is useful for planning, but an employer may calculate withholding differently under IRS Publication 15-T. For two jobs, a working spouse, bonuses, or a midyear change, use the IRS Tax Withholding Estimator and read our 2026 W-4 guide.

States with no income tax

If you live in Alaska, Florida, Nevada, New Hampshire, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, or Wyoming, enter 0% for state tax. Note that some of these states have other taxes (sales, property) that may be higher to compensate.

Frequently asked questions

How is federal income tax estimated from my paycheck?

The calculator annualizes wages, subtracts eligible pre-tax deductions and the 2026 standard deduction, applies the federal brackets, and divides the result across your pay periods. Optional W-4 dollar adjustments can raise or lower the estimate.

What is FICA tax in 2026?

Employee FICA is Social Security tax at 6.2% up to $184,500 of wages plus Medicare tax at 1.45% on all wages. Employers also withhold an additional 0.9% Medicare tax from wages they pay above $200,000 during the year.

What are pre-tax deductions?

Pre-tax deductions are amounts taken from gross pay before certain taxes. Common examples include traditional 401(k) or 403(b) contributions, qualifying health premiums, HSA contributions, and FSA contributions. Their FICA treatment differs, so this calculator conservatively applies the entered amount to federal and state taxable income but not to Social Security or Medicare wages.

What is the difference between tax bracket and effective tax rate?

Your marginal rate is the bracket your highest dollar of income falls into. Your effective rate is the average across all brackets — always lower than your marginal rate. This calculator shows your effective total tax rate.

How can I increase my take-home pay?

Review eligible benefit contributions and your W-4 to avoid unnecessary over-withholding. The current W-4 no longer uses allowances; it uses dollar adjustments in Steps 3 and 4. Lower withholding can increase each paycheck but may reduce your refund or create a balance due.
