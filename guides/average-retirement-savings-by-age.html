Average Retirement Savings by Age 2026: Are You on Track?

See what Americans actually have saved at every age — and the benchmarks you should actually be hitting.

Average vs Median: Why the Difference Matters

When you see "average retirement savings," remember: a handful of millionaires pull the average way up. The median (middle value) tells a more accurate story of what typical Americans have saved. Both numbers are shown below.

Quick reality check: The median American between 55–64 has only about $87,000 in retirement savings — far below what's needed. You're not alone if you feel behind, but the time to act is now.

The 4% Rule: How Much Do You Actually Need?

The 4% rule says you can withdraw 4% of your portfolio per year in retirement without running out of money over a 30-year period. This means:

Retirement Number = Annual Expenses × 25

Spend $40,000/year → Need: $1,000,000
Spend $60,000/year → Need: $1,500,000
Spend $80,000/year → Need: $2,000,000
Spend $100,000/year → Need: $2,500,000

Don't forget to subtract Social Security income from your expenses. If you'll receive $24,000/year in Social Security, your portfolio only needs to cover the rest.

What to Do If You're Behind

🎯
Get the Full Match First
Always contribute enough to capture 100% of your employer 401(k) match. It's a guaranteed 50–100% return on day one.
📈
Maximize Catch-Up Contributions
If you're 50+, use the extra $7,500 in your 401(k) and extra $1,000 in your IRA. At 7% returns, $7,500/year for 15 years = $185,000.
💡
Delay Retirement 2–3 Years
Working until 67 instead of 64 gives you 3 more years of contributions, 3 fewer years of withdrawals, and higher Social Security benefits.
📉
Reduce Retirement Spending
Cutting $10,000/year in retirement expenses reduces your required nest egg by $250,000. Downsizing, relocating, or reducing subscriptions all count.
🏠
Count Home Equity
A paid-off home eliminates your biggest expense or can be converted to income via downsizing or a reverse mortgage. Include it in your plan.
📋
Optimize Social Security
Delaying Social Security from 62 to 70 increases your monthly benefit by roughly 76%. Every year after 62 adds about 6–8% permanently.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I have saved for retirement by age 40?
Fidelity recommends 3x your salary by age 40. If you earn $70,000, aim for $210,000. The average 401(k) balance for 40-49 year olds is around $97,600, but the median is only $38,900.
What is the average 401k balance by age?
Average balances range from $7,300 (under 25) to $272,500 (65+). Medians are 50–70% lower. Most Americans are significantly behind the recommended benchmarks.
How much do I need to retire?
A common benchmark is 25x your annual expenses (the 4% rule). If you spend $60,000/year, you need $1.5 million. Subtract expected Social Security income from your annual need first.
What if I am behind on retirement savings?
Start by maximizing your 401(k) match, then open a Roth IRA. If you're over 50, use catch-up contributions. Delaying retirement by 2–3 years has an outsized positive impact on retirement security.
Is it too late to start saving for retirement at 50?
No. At 50 with $0 saved, contributing the maximum ($31,000/year in 401k + $8,000 IRA = $39,000/year) for 15 years at 7% returns gives you roughly $960,000 by age 65. Start now.
