How to Build Credit from Scratch (2026):
Step-by-Step Guide

No credit history? Here's exactly how to go from zero to 700+ — secured cards, credit-builder loans, the authorized user trick, and a realistic 12-month timeline.

📅 Updated June 2026 ⏱ 7 min read 📚 Sources: CFPB, FICO, Experian

Having no credit history is almost as limiting as having bad credit — lenders, landlords, and even employers check credit. The good news: building credit from scratch is entirely achievable in 12-24 months with the right moves, and you don't need to take on any meaningful debt to do it.

Step-by-Step: Building Credit from Zero

1

Become an Authorized User (Fastest Start)

Ask a parent, spouse, or trusted family member to add you as an authorized user on their oldest credit card with low utilization. That account's full history appears on your credit report immediately — potentially giving you years of credit history overnight. You don't need to use the card or even hold it.

Timeline: Score may appear within 30-60 days
2

Open a Secured Credit Card

Deposit $200-$500 as collateral and use the card for small, recurring purchases (Netflix, gas, groceries). Pay the full balance every month before the due date — never carry a balance. Best issuers for building credit: Discover it® Secured, Capital One Platinum Secured, Citi Secured Mastercard. After 6-12 months of on-time payments, most upgrade you to unsecured and return your deposit.

Timeline: Score generates after 6 months of history
3

Get a Credit-Builder Loan (Optional but Powerful)

Credit unions and some community banks offer credit-builder loans specifically designed for this purpose. You make monthly payments of $25-$75/month; the money goes into a savings account you receive at the end. This adds an installment loan to your file (good for credit mix) and demonstrates on-time payment history.

Timeline: Starts reporting immediately; full benefit at 12 months
4

Add Positive Data with Experian Boost

Experian Boost lets you add utility, phone, and streaming service payment history to your credit file for free. This can add 10-20 points and helps people with thin credit files the most. Note: only affects Experian FICO scores.

Timeline: Immediate effect on Experian score
5

Apply for a Second Card at 12 Months

After 12 months of history, you'll likely qualify for a basic unsecured card. A second card doubles your total credit limit, dropping your utilization ratio. Apply for one card — not multiple — to minimize hard inquiry impact.

Target score by this point: 660-700

Dos and Don'ts

✅ Do These

  • Pay every bill on time, every month — set autopay
  • Keep card utilization under 10% of the limit
  • Check your credit report free at AnnualCreditReport.com
  • Dispute errors on your credit report immediately
  • Keep your oldest accounts open even if unused
  • Request credit limit increases after 6+ months

❌ Don't Do These

  • Apply for multiple cards at once (multiple hard inquiries)
  • Carry a balance on your credit card (costs interest, no credit benefit)
  • Close your oldest credit account
  • Max out your credit cards even if you pay them off
  • Miss a single payment — even one 30-day late drops your score 60-100 points
  • Pay a "credit repair" company — you can do everything yourself for free

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to build credit from scratch? +
You need 6 months of credit history to generate a FICO score. With the right strategies (secured card + authorized user + on-time payments), most people reach 650-680 within 6-12 months and 700+ within 12-24 months.
What is the fastest way to build credit? +
The fastest combination: (1) Become an authorized user on a family member's old, low-utilization card — adds history immediately. (2) Open a secured credit card and pay it in full monthly. (3) Get a credit-builder loan. These three together can produce a scoreable file within 1-2 months and 650+ within 6 months.
What is a secured credit card? +
A secured card requires a cash deposit ($200-$500) as your credit limit. It works like a regular card and reports to all three bureaus. After 6-12 months of on-time payments, most issuers upgrade you to an unsecured card and return your deposit. Good options: Discover it Secured, Capital One Platinum Secured.
Can I build credit without a credit card? +
Yes. Credit-builder loans from credit unions are specifically designed for this. You can also add rent payments via Experian Boost or RentReporters. And becoming an authorized user doesn't require you to use any card at all.
What hurts your credit score most? +
In order: (1) Missing payments — payment history is 35% of FICO. One 30-day late can drop you 60-100 points. (2) High utilization — over 30% hurts significantly. (3) Too many hard inquiries at once. (4) Closing old accounts. Focus obsessively on #1 and #2.
