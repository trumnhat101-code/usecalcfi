Average Net Worth by Age (2026):
Are You On Track?

Federal Reserve benchmarks for average and median net worth at every age — plus why median matters more, and what moves the needle fastest.

📅 Updated June 2026 ⏱ 7 min read 📚 Sources: Federal Reserve Survey of Consumer Finances, Fidelity

Comparing your net worth to your peers is one of the most useful financial reality checks you can do. The Federal Reserve's Survey of Consumer Finances (SCF) — collected every 3 years — is the gold standard for these benchmarks. Here's what Americans actually have at every age.

Average vs. Median: Why the Difference Matters

The average net worth is skewed significantly upward by billionaires and ultra-high-net-worth households. The median represents the person right in the middle — a much better benchmark for most Americans. If your net worth is near or above the median for your age group, you're doing better than half of Americans.

What Counts as Net Worth?

Assets (what you own):

  • Home equity (market value minus mortgage balance)
  • Retirement accounts (401k, IRA, Roth IRA, pension cash value)
  • Brokerage/investment accounts
  • Cash, savings, checking accounts
  • Vehicle value (current market value)
  • Business ownership stake
  • Other property and valuables

Liabilities (what you owe):

  • Mortgage balance
  • Auto loan balances
  • Student loan balances
  • Credit card debt
  • Personal loans
  • Any other outstanding debts

Net Worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities. Use our Net Worth Calculator to add it all up in one place.

Why Your Net Worth Jumps After 45

The median net worth roughly doubles from the 35-44 group to the 45-54 group. The main drivers:

  • Home equity growth: 10-20 years of mortgage payments + home appreciation
  • Compounding on retirement accounts: 401k contributions from age 25 have been compounding for 20+ years
  • Peak earnings years: Most workers hit peak income in their 40s and 50s
  • Debt payoff: Student loans and early consumer debt are paid off
  • Lower housing costs: Mortgage often paid down significantly, freeing cash flow

How to Build Net Worth Faster

  1. Eliminate high-interest debt first. Paying off 20% credit card debt is a guaranteed 20% return — better than any investment.
  2. Max employer 401(k) match. Free money that compounds tax-deferred.
  3. Buy a home when ready. Homeowners have a median net worth ~40x higher than renters ($396K vs $10K per Fed data).
  4. Invest consistently in low-cost index funds. Time in market beats timing the market. Even $200/month from age 25 at 7% = $525,000 by 65.
  5. Increase income. Net worth grows fastest when you combine growing income with controlled spending.
  6. Keep lifestyle inflation in check. Lifestyle creep — spending more as you earn more — is the biggest wealth killer in peak earning years.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average net worth by age? +
Federal Reserve SCF data: Under 35: avg $183K, median $39K. 35-44: avg $549K, median $135K. 45-54: avg $975K, median $247K. 55-64: avg $1.57M, median $364K. 65-74: avg $1.79M, median $410K. Use median — average is skewed by billionaires.
What is net worth? +
Net worth = total assets minus total liabilities. Assets: home equity, retirement accounts, investments, cash, vehicles. Liabilities: mortgage, auto loans, student loans, credit card debt. A positive net worth means you own more than you owe.
What should my net worth be at 30? +
The median for under-35 Americans is ~$39,000. Fidelity targets 1x your salary saved in retirement by 30. Don't panic if you're below — student debt and early career make the 20s very hard for net worth building. Starting to invest at 25-30 is still early enough for compound interest to work powerfully.
How do I calculate my net worth? +
Add up all assets (home equity, retirement accounts, investments, cash, vehicles). Subtract all liabilities (mortgage balance, loans, credit card debt). Use our Net Worth Calculator for a guided walkthrough.
What is a good net worth at 40? +
The median for 35-44 year olds is ~$135,000. Fidelity targets 3x annual salary in retirement savings by 40. On $75K salary: $225K in retirement accounts. Adding home equity and other assets is on top of that benchmark.
