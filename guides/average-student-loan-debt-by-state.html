⚠️ The Hidden Cost of Deferment & Forbearance

During deferment or forbearance, interest continues to accrue on unsubsidized federal loans. This accrued interest then capitalizes — it gets added to your principal — meaning you pay interest on interest for the rest of your loan term.

🧰 4 Strategies to Pay Off Student Loans Faster

1. Make Payments During Grace Period & In School

The 6-month grace period after graduation is a golden opportunity. Interest accrues during this time, and any payments you make go straight to principal. Making even $100–$200/month during school on unsubsidized loans can save thousands over the life of the loan.

2. Pay More Than the Minimum — Target the Principal

On a $38,500 loan at 6.53%, adding just $100/month to the standard payment pays off the loan in about 7.5 years instead of 10 — saving over $4,500 in interest. Specify "apply to principal" when making extra payments.

3. Refinance (After Weighing Federal Protections)

Refinancing federal loans to a lower private rate can save money — but you permanently lose access to income-driven repayment, Public Service Loan Forgiveness, and federal forbearance. Only refinance federal loans if you have stable income, no plans to use PSLF, and can secure a meaningfully lower rate.

4. Pursue Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)

If you work for a qualifying employer (government, nonprofits), 120 qualifying payments on an IDR plan result in complete forgiveness of remaining federal balances. For borrowers with high debt and lower salaries — especially those in DC, Maryland, and Virginia — PSLF can be transformative.

Avoid these mistakes: Ignoring loans during the grace period (interest accrues), choosing a 25-year extended plan without running the numbers, missing IDR recertification deadlines, and refinancing federal loans without fully understanding what you're giving up.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average student loan debt in the US?
The average federal student loan balance per borrower in 2026 is approximately $38,500, based on Federal Student Aid portfolio data. Total outstanding student loan debt in the U.S. has surpassed $1.77 trillion across approximately 43 million borrowers.
Which state has the highest student loan debt?
The District of Columbia has the highest average student loan balance at $54,200, reflecting the high concentration of graduate and professional degree holders. Georgia ($45,600) and Maryland ($43,500) rank second and third, driven by higher rates of graduate school attendance and higher tuition at private institutions.
Which state has the lowest student loan debt?
Utah has the lowest average student loan balance at $30,400, followed by Wyoming ($31,800) and Iowa ($32,200). Utah benefits from lower tuition at state schools and Brigham Young University's below-market tuition, as well as cultural values around debt avoidance.
What is the monthly payment on average student loan debt?
On the national average balance of $38,500 at the 2026 federal rate of 6.53%, the standard 10-year monthly payment is approximately $437. Over 10 years, you'll pay roughly $52,400 total — about $13,900 in interest. Extending to 20 years lowers the monthly payment to about $290 but adds more than $16,000 in extra interest.
What happens to student loans during deferment?
During deferment or forbearance on unsubsidized federal loans, interest continues to accrue. On the average $38,500 balance at 6.53%, deferring for 12 months adds approximately $2,514 in accrued interest — which then capitalizes (gets added to principal), increasing your long-term cost by several thousand dollars more over the remaining repayment term.
What is the federal student loan interest rate in 2026?
For 2026, federal student loan interest rates are 6.53% for undergraduate Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized Loans, 8.08% for graduate Unsubsidized Loans, and 9.08% for Direct PLUS Loans. Rates are fixed for the life of each loan disbursed in that academic year and are set annually based on the 10-year Treasury note yield.

📐 Methodology & Data Sources

  • Average balance data: State-level figures derived from the Education Data Initiative's Student Loan Debt by State dataset (2024), cross-referenced with Federal Student Aid's H4Y Portfolio Summary (FY2025) and adjusted for 2026 based on historical growth trends of approximately 2–3% annually.
  • Interest rate: 6.53% reflects the Department of Education's fixed rate for Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized Loans disbursed in the 2025–2026 academic year, set per the HEROES Act formula (10-year Treasury + 2.05% add-on for undergrad loans).
  • Monthly payment calculations: Standard amortization formula PMT = P × r(1+r)⊃n / ((1+r)⊃n − 1), where r = monthly rate (6.53%/12), n = number of months, P = balance. 10-year = 120 months; 20-year = 240 months.
  • Deferment cost: Assumes simple interest accrual during 12-month period at 6.53% annual rate on the national average balance, with capitalization at end of deferment period and standard 10-year repayment on the new balance.
  • National totals: $1.77 trillion total and 43 million borrowers sourced from Federal Student Aid's Q1 FY2026 Portfolio Summary.
