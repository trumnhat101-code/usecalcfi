How Much House Can I Afford on a $60,000 Salary?

🗓️ Last reviewed: June 2026 · Method: 28% front-end DTI rule

On a $60,000 salary, you can afford a home of roughly $180,000 with 10% down, or about $211,000 with 20% down — using the 28% rule. That works out to a maximum housing payment of about $1,400/month (principal, interest, taxes & insurance) at a 6.5% rate.

The 28/36 Rule Explained

Lenders use two limits. The front-end ratio says your housing payment should stay at or below 28% of gross monthly income — about $1,400/month on a $60,000 salary (gross monthly income of $5,000). The back-end ratio says your total debt (housing plus car loans, student loans, credit cards, etc.) should stay under 36%. If you carry other debt, your affordable home price will be lower than the figures above.

⚖️ These are estimates based on common assumptions, not a loan offer or financial advice. Your actual approval depends on credit score, debts, down payment, and lender. Always get a real pre-approval.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much house can I afford on a $60,000 salary?

Roughly $180,000 with 10% down or $211,000 with 20% down, assuming a 6.5% rate and no other debts — a maximum payment of about $1,400/month.

What mortgage payment can I afford on $60,000?

About $1,400/month using the 28% rule, which is 28% of your $5,000 gross monthly income.

Is a $60,000 salary enough to buy a house?

In most U.S. markets, yes — a $60,000 salary supports a home around $180,000–$211,000. In high-cost metros it may only cover a condo or require a larger down payment.
