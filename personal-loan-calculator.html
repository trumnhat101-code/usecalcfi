How to use this personal loan calculator

Enter your desired loan amount, the APR offered by your lender, and choose a repayment term. If your lender charges an origination fee, add it to see your true APR. The calculator shows your monthly payment, total interest, and total repayment cost.

Shorter vs. longer loan term

A shorter term means higher monthly payments but less total interest. A longer term lowers your monthly payment but you'll pay significantly more over the life of the loan. For example, a $15,000 loan at 12% APR: 36 months = $498/mo, $942 interest; 60 months = $333/mo, $2,996 interest.

Frequently asked questions

What can I use a personal loan for?

Personal loans can be used for debt consolidation, home improvement, medical expenses, major purchases, weddings, or emergencies. Most lenders prohibit using them for investments, gambling, or illegal activities.

Secured vs. unsecured personal loan?

Most personal loans are unsecured (no collateral required), which is why rates are higher than mortgage or auto loans. Secured personal loans require collateral (savings account, vehicle) and offer lower rates but risk losing the asset if you default.

How does an origination fee affect my loan?

An origination fee (typically 1–8% of the loan) is often deducted from your disbursement. A $15,000 loan with a 3% fee means you receive $14,550 but owe $15,000. This calculator includes the fee in your true APR calculation.
