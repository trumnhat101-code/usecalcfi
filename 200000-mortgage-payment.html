$200,000 Mortgage Payment

🗓️ Last reviewed: June 2026 · Source: standard amortization formula

A $200,000 mortgage costs about $1,264 per month at a 6.5% interest rate over 30 years (principal & interest). With typical property tax and home insurance, the full monthly payment is roughly $1,589. A 15-year loan raises the payment to about $1,742/month but cuts total interest by more than half.

Total Interest on a $200,000 Mortgage

The longer your term, the lower the monthly payment but the more interest you pay overall. At 6.5%, a 30-year $200,000 loan costs about $255,000 in total interest, while a 15-year loan costs about $114,000 — a difference of roughly $141,000.

What Income Do You Need for a $200,000 Mortgage?

Lenders generally want your total housing payment to stay at or below 28% of your gross monthly income (the front-end DTI rule). With a full payment of about $1,589/month (principal, interest, taxes and insurance), that points to roughly $70,000 in annual income. You may qualify with less if you have little other debt, or need more if you carry car loans, student loans, or credit card balances.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is a $200,000 mortgage payment per month?

About $1,264/month at 6.5% over 30 years for principal and interest. With typical property tax and insurance, the full monthly payment is roughly $1,589.

How much is a $200,000 mortgage at 7%?

About $1,331/month over 30 years, with roughly $279,000 in total interest over the life of the loan.

What is the 15-year payment on a $200,000 mortgage?

About $1,742/month at 6.5% — higher monthly, but total interest drops to about $114,000 versus $255,000 on a 30-year loan.
