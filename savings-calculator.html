Make your savings work harder

The difference between a 0.01% traditional savings account and a 4.5% high-yield savings account is enormous over time. On $10,000 with $300/month for 5 years, you'd earn about $60 in interest at the traditional rate — vs. over $3,500 at 4.5%.

High-yield savings accounts (HYSAs)

Online banks like Marcus (Goldman Sachs), Ally, and CIT Bank offer HYSAs with 4–5% APY — 10–50x more than most traditional banks. They're FDIC insured and work identically to a regular savings account. The only difference: you manage them online.

When to use savings vs. investments

  • Savings account: Emergency fund (3–6 months expenses), money needed within 1–3 years
  • Investments: Money you won't need for 5+ years (retirement, long-term goals)
  • CD (Certificate of Deposit): Locked savings for a fixed term at a guaranteed rate — good when you know you won't need the money

Frequently asked questions

What is a high-yield savings account?

An HYSA pays significantly more than a traditional savings account — often 4–5% APY vs. 0.01–0.05%. FDIC insured, no risk. Available at online banks like Marcus, Ally, and CIT Bank.

How much should I keep in savings?

Keep 3–6 months of living expenses in an emergency fund. Beyond that, money you won't need for 5+ years is better invested in index funds for higher long-term returns.
