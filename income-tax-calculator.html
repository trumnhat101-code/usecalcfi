2026 Federal income tax brackets

The US uses a progressive tax system — each bracket rate applies only to income within that range, not your entire income. Here are the 2026 brackets for single filers:

  • 10% — on taxable income from $0 to $12,400
  • 12% — on taxable income from $12,401 to $50,400
  • 22% — on taxable income from $50,401 to $105,700
  • 24% — on taxable income from $105,701 to $201,775
  • 32% — on taxable income from $201,776 to $256,225
  • 35% — on taxable income from $256,226 to $640,600
  • 37% — on taxable income over $640,600

Thresholds differ by filing status; the calculator automatically uses the correct 2026 table for single, married filing jointly, head of household, or married filing separately.

Standard deduction 2026

Before applying tax brackets, this calculator reduces income by the 2026 standard deduction: $16,100 for single or married filing separately, $32,200 for married filing jointly, and $24,150 for head of household. Eligible pre-tax deductions can further reduce taxable income.

Ways to legally reduce your tax bill

  • Maximize 401(k) contributions — The employee deferral limit is $24,500 in 2026.
  • Contribute to an HSA — The 2026 limits are $4,400 self-only / $8,750 family for eligible plans.
  • Itemize deductions if they exceed the standard deduction (mortgage interest, charity, state taxes)
  • Tax-loss harvesting — Offset capital gains with investment losses
  • Contribute to a Traditional IRA — Up to $7,500 in 2026 ($8,600 if age 50+) may be deductible, subject to income and plan-coverage rules.

Frequently asked questions

Does this include FICA (Social Security and Medicare)?

No — this calculator covers federal income tax only. In 2026, employee Social Security tax is 6.2% up to $184,500 of wages and Medicare tax is 1.45% on all wages. Use the Paycheck Calculator for a take-home estimate including FICA.

What is the SALT deduction cap?

For 2026, the federal itemized deduction for state and local taxes is generally capped at $40,400 ($20,200 if married filing separately), with a phase-down for higher-income taxpayers but not below $10,000. This calculator uses the standard deduction and does not model itemized deductions. See the IRS 2026 SALT correction.

When do I need to file?

The federal tax deadline is April 15 each year (or the next business day if it falls on a weekend or holiday). You can request an automatic 6-month extension by filing Form 4868, but any taxes owed are still due by the original deadline.
