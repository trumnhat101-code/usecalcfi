2025 Federal income tax brackets

The US uses a progressive tax system — each bracket rate applies only to income within that range, not your entire income. Here are the 2025 brackets for single filers:

  • 10% — on income from $0 to $11,925
  • 12% — on income from $11,926 to $48,475
  • 22% — on income from $48,476 to $103,350
  • 24% — on income from $103,351 to $197,300
  • 32% — on income from $197,301 to $250,525
  • 35% — on income from $250,526 to $626,350
  • 37% — on income over $626,350

Married filing jointly thresholds are exactly double the single thresholds for most brackets.

Standard deduction 2025

Before applying tax brackets, your taxable income is reduced by the standard deduction: $15,000 for single filers, $30,000 for married filing jointly, and $22,500 for head of household. Pre-tax deductions (401k, HSA) further reduce your taxable income.

Ways to legally reduce your tax bill

  • Maximize 401(k) contributions — Up to $23,500 in 2025 (pre-tax reduces taxable income)
  • Contribute to an HSA — $4,300 (self) / $8,550 (family) in 2025, triple tax advantage
  • Itemize deductions if they exceed the standard deduction (mortgage interest, charity, state taxes)
  • Tax-loss harvesting — Offset capital gains with investment losses
  • Contribute to a Traditional IRA — Up to $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+) may be deductible

Frequently asked questions

Does this include FICA (Social Security and Medicare)?

No — this calculator covers federal income tax only. Social Security (6.2% up to $176,100) and Medicare (1.45%) are separate taxes. Use the Paycheck Calculator for a full take-home pay estimate including FICA.

What is the SALT deduction cap?

The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (2017) caps the deduction for state and local taxes (SALT) at $10,000 per year for itemizers. This mostly affects high-income residents of high-tax states like California, New York, and New Jersey.

When do I need to file?

The federal tax deadline is April 15 each year (or the next business day if it falls on a weekend or holiday). You can request an automatic 6-month extension by filing Form 4868, but any taxes owed are still due by the original deadline.
