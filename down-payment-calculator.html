How much down payment do you need?

The minimum down payment depends on your loan type. Conventional loans go as low as 3%, but anything under 20% triggers PMI — a monthly fee that protects the lender, not you.

Loan type minimums

  • Conventional: 3–5% minimum, 20% to avoid PMI
  • FHA: 3.5% minimum (580+ credit score), 10% if score is 500–579
  • VA / USDA: 0% — no down payment required for eligible borrowers
  • Jumbo: 10–20% typically required by lenders

Closing costs: don't forget these

Beyond the down payment, plan for closing costs of 2–5% of the home price ($8,000–$20,000 on a $400k home). These are separate from your down payment and must be paid at closing.

Frequently asked questions

What is PMI and how can I avoid it?

PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is required on conventional loans when your down payment is less than 20%. It typically costs 0.5–1.5%/year (~$100–300/month). Avoid it by putting 20% down, using a VA/USDA loan, or with a piggyback loan.

Is a 20% down payment required?

No — but 20% eliminates PMI and gets you better rates. Many buyers use 5–10% and accept PMI temporarily. PMI cancels automatically when equity reaches 22%.

How Much Down Payment Do You Actually Need?

The "20% down" rule is a guideline, not a requirement. Many loan programs accept much less — but a lower down payment usually means paying Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI), a higher interest rate, or both. Here's what the major loan types require:

  • Conventional loans: As low as 3% (first-time buyers) or 5% (repeat buyers). PMI required under 20%.
  • FHA loans: 3.5% with 580+ credit score, 10% with 500–579 score. Mortgage insurance required for life of loan if down payment is under 10%.
  • VA loans: 0% down for eligible veterans and active duty service members. No PMI ever.
  • USDA loans: 0% down in eligible rural areas for buyers within income limits.

The True Cost of PMI

PMI typically costs 0.5–1.5% of the loan amount annually. On a $300,000 loan, that's $1,500–$4,500 per year ($125–$375/month). Unlike mortgage interest, PMI is not tax-deductible for most borrowers and builds you no equity. Once your loan-to-value ratio reaches 80%, you can request cancellation — lenders must automatically cancel at 78% LTV.

Don't Forget Closing Costs

Beyond the down payment, buyers typically pay 2–5% of the home price in closing costs: lender fees, title insurance, appraisal, prepaid taxes and insurance. On a $350,000 home, closing costs often run $7,000–$17,500. Budget for both — many first-time buyers save for the down payment but are caught off guard by closing costs.
