How the 2025 federal tax refund works

Throughout the year, your employer withholds federal income tax from each paycheck based on the W-4 you filed. At tax time, your actual tax liability is calculated. If you had more withheld than you owe, you get a refund. If less, you owe the difference.

Getting a big refund isn't always good

A large refund means you gave the government an interest-free loan all year. Adjusting your W-4 to withhold less gives you more take-home pay each paycheck — and you can invest or save that money instead. Aim to come close to breaking even at tax time.

Frequently asked questions

How is my tax refund calculated?

Refund = federal tax withheld − actual tax liability. Your liability comes from applying 2025 IRS brackets to your taxable income, then subtracting credits.

Why is my refund smaller than last year?

Common reasons: you updated your W-4, got a raise, had less withholding from a new employer, or your credits changed. A smaller refund often means more accurate withholding throughout the year.
