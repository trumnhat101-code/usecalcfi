How an HSA Works

A Health Savings Account (HSA) is only available to people enrolled in a High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP). It provides three layers of tax benefits — making it arguably the most tax-advantaged account available. For 2026, an HDHP is a plan with a deductible of at least $1,650 (individual) or $3,300 (family).

2026 HSA Contribution Limits

  • Self-only coverage: $4,400
  • Family coverage: $8,750
  • Catch-up (age 55+): additional $1,000

Employer contributions count toward these limits. You can contribute up to the deadline of your tax filing date (usually April 15).

The "Stealth IRA" Strategy

Advanced users pay current medical expenses out-of-pocket, keep the receipts, and let their HSA grow invested for decades. There is no time limit to reimburse yourself — you can withdraw tax-free for a medical expense from 10 years ago. This effectively converts your HSA into a tax-free retirement account when used strategically.
