What Is Net Worth?

Net worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities. It's the single most important number for measuring financial health. A positive net worth means you own more than you owe. A negative net worth (common in your 20s with student loans) means you're building from a deficit.

Net Worth Benchmarks by Age

According to the Federal Reserve's Survey of Consumer Finances, median US household net worth by age group:

  • Under 35: ~$39,000 median / ~$183,000 mean
  • 35–44: ~$135,000 median / ~$549,000 mean
  • 45–54: ~$247,000 median / ~$975,000 mean
  • 55–64: ~$364,000 median / ~$1.57M mean
  • 65–74: ~$409,000 median / ~$1.79M mean

Mean is higher than median because wealthy households skew the average.

How to Build Net Worth Faster

The fastest levers: (1) Max retirement contributions for tax-deferred compounding. (2) Pay down high-interest debt — guaranteed "return" equal to the interest rate. (3) Increase income and invest the difference. (4) Build home equity through extra principal payments. (5) Track annually — awareness drives better decisions.
