How to use this retirement calculator

Enter your current age and the age at which you plan to retire. Add your current total savings across all retirement accounts (401k, IRA, Roth IRA, brokerage), your monthly contribution, and the annual return you expect. Set an inflation rate (3% is a common long-term assumption) and your target monthly spending in retirement. Click Calculate to see your projected balance, monthly income, and how close you are to your goal.

How your retirement balance is calculated

Two formulas combine to project your balance:

Future value of current savings: FV = PV × (1 + r)^n

Future value of monthly contributions: FV = PMT × ((1 + r)^n − 1) / r

  • PV = current savings
  • PMT = monthly contribution
  • r = monthly return rate (annual % ÷ 12)
  • n = months until retirement

The total projected balance = FV of savings + FV of contributions.

The 4% rule — estimating monthly income

The 4% rule (also called the "safe withdrawal rate") says you can withdraw 4% of your portfolio in year one of retirement and adjust for inflation annually, with a high probability of not running out of money over a 30-year retirement. The monthly income estimate = Portfolio × 4% ÷ 12.

Some financial planners suggest a more conservative 3–3.5% withdrawal rate for retirements lasting 35+ years or in low-return environments.

Example

A 35-year-old with $50,000 saved, contributing $500/month for 30 years at 7% return: projected balance ≈ $952,000, monthly income (4% rule) ≈ $3,173/month. To cover $5,000/month, the target portfolio = $1.5M.

Tips to maximize your retirement savings

  • Maximize your 401(k) — The 2025 employee contribution limit is $23,500 ($31,000 if 50+). At minimum, contribute enough to get your full employer match — that's a 50–100% instant return.
  • Open a Roth IRA — Contributions grow tax-free. 2025 limit: $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+). Income limits apply.
  • Start early — time is your biggest asset — $200/month invested from age 25 to 65 at 7% grows to ~$525,000. Starting at 35 gives only ~$243,000.
  • Increase contributions automatically — Set your 401(k) to increase by 1% each year. You likely won't notice the difference in your paycheck.
  • Keep fees low — A 1% annual fee can reduce your final balance by 25% over 30 years. Use low-cost index funds.

Frequently asked questions

Does this calculator include Social Security?

No — this calculator projects only personal savings. Social Security can significantly supplement your retirement income. Use the SSA's my Social Security tool to estimate your benefit, then subtract it from your monthly income goal before using this calculator.

What if I retire early?

Early retirement (before 59½) means you can't access traditional 401k/IRA funds without a 10% penalty (with some exceptions). You'll need taxable brokerage savings, a Roth conversion ladder, or other strategies. The required portfolio also grows larger the longer your retirement lasts.

Should I use real or nominal returns?

This calculator shows both: the projected nominal balance (raw dollars) and the balance in today's purchasing power (real value after inflation). Planning in real terms gives a more accurate picture of what your money will actually buy.
