The Math Behind Extra Mortgage Payments

Mortgage interest is calculated monthly on your outstanding principal balance. Every dollar of extra principal payment immediately reduces the balance, which reduces next month's interest charge. That freed-up interest capacity goes toward more principal in every subsequent payment — creating a compounding acceleration effect.

This is why the biggest savings come from extra payments made early in the loan when the balance (and thus monthly interest) is highest. An extra $200 in month 1 of a 30-year loan saves far more than the same $200 in year 25.

The one extra payment per year strategy

Making 13 monthly payments per year (one extra payment annually) is a popular payoff strategy. On a $300,000 loan at 7%, this single extra payment per year cuts your 30-year mortgage to approximately 24.5 years and saves over $60,000 in interest. You can achieve this by dividing your monthly payment by 12 and adding that amount to each payment.

Should You Pay Off Your Mortgage Early or Invest?

This is the most common question homeowners face. The math-optimal answer depends on your mortgage rate vs. expected investment returns. With a 7% mortgage, paying it down is a guaranteed 7% return. The S&P 500 has historically returned ~10% annually before inflation — suggesting investing may win long-term. But market returns aren't guaranteed, while your mortgage interest rate savings are.

The balanced approach most financial advisors recommend: first max out tax-advantaged accounts (401k employer match, Roth IRA, HSA), then split additional savings between investing and extra mortgage payments. You get market upside while steadily reducing debt exposure.

Biweekly Payments vs. Monthly Extra Payments

Paying half your monthly payment every two weeks results in 26 half-payments (13 full payments) per year — automatically making one extra payment annually without thinking about it. Many lenders offer this option. Be sure your lender applies biweekly payments directly to principal and not just holds them until month-end, which would negate the benefit.

Frequently asked questions

How much do extra mortgage payments really save?

On a $300,000 mortgage at 7% with 30 years remaining, paying $200 extra per month saves over $62,000 in interest and pays off the loan 6 years early. Even $100/month extra saves $35,000 and cuts 4 years off.

Is it better to pay extra on a mortgage or invest?

If your mortgage rate is below expected market returns (~10%), investing may generate more wealth long-term. But paying down mortgage offers a guaranteed, risk-free return equal to your rate. Many advisors recommend maxing tax-advantaged accounts first, then split remaining savings between both.

Should I pay off my mortgage early or invest?

Max tax-advantaged accounts first (401k match, IRA, HSA), then use remaining extra cash toward the mortgage. Your mortgage rate is your guaranteed return — compare it to investment alternatives when deciding how to split.
