How to use this mortgage calculator

Enter your home price and down payment, choose a loan term, and add your interest rate. Optionally include property tax, home insurance and HOA dues to see your full monthly payment (PITI).

How is a monthly mortgage payment calculated?

Principal & interest are computed with the standard amortization formula:

M = P · r · (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n − 1)

  • P = loan amount (home price − down payment)
  • r = monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12)
  • n = number of payments (years × 12)

Taxes, insurance and HOA are added on top to get your total monthly payment.

Example

A $320,000 loan at 6.5% for 30 years ≈ $2,022/month in principal & interest, with about $408,000 paid in interest over the life of the loan.

Frequently asked questions

What is PITI?

Principal, Interest, Taxes and Insurance — the four parts of a typical monthly payment.

How can I lower my monthly payment?

Increase your down payment, choose a longer term, improve your credit score for a lower rate, or shop multiple lenders.

Do I need 20% down?

Not always — many loans allow 3–5% down, though under 20% usually adds PMI. A bigger down payment reduces interest paid.

Principal vs Interest: Where Your Payment Goes

In the early years of a 30-year mortgage, the vast majority of your payment goes to interest — not building equity. On a $320,000 loan at 7%, your first payment of ~$2,130 breaks down as: $1,867 interest, $264 principal. By year 15, it flips: about $1,200 interest, $930 principal.

This is why extra payments made early have an outsized impact. An extra $100/month on a $320,000 loan at 7% cuts your payoff time by about 4 years and saves roughly $68,000 in interest.

Understanding Your Results

Your monthly payment breaks into two parts: principal (the portion reducing your loan balance) and interest (the lender's fee for the loan). In the early years of a 30-year mortgage, the vast majority of each payment goes to interest — on a $300,000 loan at 6.72%, your first payment sends only about $372 to principal and $1,680 to interest.

30-Year vs. 15-Year Mortgage

A 15-year mortgage typically offers a lower rate (usually 0.5–0.75% less) and cuts total interest dramatically. On a $300,000 loan, a 30-year at 6.72% costs about $398,000 in interest. A 15-year at 6.0% costs about $155,000 in interest — saving over $240,000. The tradeoff: the monthly payment is roughly 40% higher. The 15-year works best for borrowers with stable high income who prioritize building equity fast.

What the Monthly Payment Doesn't Include

Lenders require property taxes (typically 0.5–2% of home value annually), homeowners insurance (~$150–$250/month), and PMI if your down payment is under 20% (typically 0.5–1.5% of loan amount annually). These can add $400–$800/month to the base payment — always budget for the full PITI amount, not just principal and interest.

How to Lower Your Monthly Payment

  • Put more down: A larger down payment reduces the loan amount directly and eliminates PMI at 20%+
  • Improve your credit score: Even 20–30 points can qualify you for a lower rate, saving thousands over the loan term
  • Shop multiple lenders: Rate quotes can vary by 0.5–1% between lenders for the same borrower profile — that's $50–$100/month on a typical mortgage
  • Buy mortgage points: Pay 1% of loan upfront to reduce rate by ~0.25%. Break-even is typically 4–6 years
  • Choose a longer term: A 30-year has a lower payment than a 15-year, though total interest is much higher
