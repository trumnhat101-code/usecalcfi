How much car can I afford?

Use the 20/4/10 rule: put at least 20% down, finance for no more than 4 years (48 months), and keep your total monthly car expenses (payment + insurance) under 10% of your gross monthly income.

What's a good car payment?

Most financial experts say your car payment should not exceed 10–15% of your monthly take-home pay. If you earn $5,000/month after taxes, aim to keep your payment under $500–$750.

Frequently asked questions

Does a bigger down payment always make sense?

Yes, if you have the cash. A larger down payment reduces your loan amount, lowers your monthly payment, and reduces the risk of being underwater (owing more than the car is worth). Cars depreciate 15–25% in the first year alone — a solid down payment protects you.

Should I finance through a dealer or my bank?

Always get pre-approved by your bank or credit union before visiting a dealer. This gives you a rate benchmark to negotiate against. Dealers often mark up the rate they receive from lenders — sometimes by 1–3%. Pre-approval puts you in control.

What credit score do I need for a good car loan rate?

Borrowers with 720+ FICO typically qualify for the best rates (4.5–6% APR). Scores of 660–719 get mid-range rates. Below 620, expect rates of 10–15%+ or difficulty qualifying without a co-signer. Improving your score even 30–40 points can save thousands over the life of the loan.
