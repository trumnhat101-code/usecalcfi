How much car can I afford?

The informal 20/4/10 guideline suggests 20% down, a term of 48 months or less, and keeping the payment plus insurance below 10% of gross monthly income. It is a budgeting guardrail, not a lender rule. See the worked affordability examples before choosing a price.

Frequently asked questions

Does this calculator include sales tax, trade-in value, or dealer fees?

No. It deliberately isolates price, down payment, APR, and term. Use the Auto Loan Calculator when you need sales tax and trade-in value included.

Can I calculate a 0% APR car loan?

Yes. Enter 0 for APR. The payment becomes the amount financed divided by the number of months, with $0 total interest.

What loan term should I choose?

Compare the shortest term whose payment fits your budget. Longer terms reduce the monthly bill but increase total interest and can keep you in negative equity longer.

Does a larger down payment lower the payment?

Yes. It reduces the amount financed, monthly payment, and interest. Keep enough cash for emergencies instead of putting every available dollar into the car.

How to compare car loan offers

The APR entered above should come from an actual lender quote. The CFPB recommends shopping before visiting the dealership and comparing the full loan terms, not only the monthly payment.

  • Request the out-the-door price so taxes, registration, dealer fees, and add-ons are visible.
  • Get preapproved by more than one lender within a focused shopping window.
  • Compare APR, amount financed, term, and total of payments on the disclosures.
  • Keep add-ons separate; warranties and protection products increase the amount financed when rolled into the loan.

The APR comparison generated by this calculator uses the same amount financed and term for every row, so it isolates the cost of the rate itself.
